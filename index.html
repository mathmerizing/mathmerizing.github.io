Solving the Schrödinger Equation with deal.ii
authors:    Leon Samolik, Max Schröder, Julian Roth

This group project is the result of a lecture on programming finite elements in C++ held at the Leibniz Universität Hannover in the winter semester 2019/20. We decided to simulate the quantum harmonic oscillator numerically. For that purpose, we discretized the Schrödinger equation with the harmonic oscillator as the potential, i.e.


To apply the finite element method, we discretized the time derivative with the one step θ-scheme. The resulting equation was split into its real and imaginary part and then transformed into a weak form by multiplication with a test function and integration by parts. In a 2D setting a direct solver, like the LU decomposition, turned out to be sufficient to solve our linear equation system. Additionally, the inverse only needed to be calculated once, since the system matrix remained constant throughout time. Due to the fact that we decided to use Dirichlet boundary conditions instead of periodic boundary conditions, our simulation always converged against the same eigenstate, even when other parameters where changed slightly.
